# Question Bank

Ask fewer questions than seem tempting. Prefer one high-value question over a checklist.

## Problem Shape

- What outcome are you actually trying to get?
- What changed recently that made this a problem now?
- Is this a one-time event or a repeating pattern?
- What have you already tried?
- What would count as a good enough resolution?

## Constraints

- What cannot change?
- What resources are limited: time, money, attention, authority, trust, data, skill, or distribution?
- What deadline matters?
- What happens if you do nothing?
- Which option is reversible?

## Stakes And Risk

- What is the downside if this goes wrong?
- Who else is affected?
- Is the cost mostly financial, reputational, emotional, technical, legal, or operational?
- What would make you regret the decision later?
- What assumption would be most damaging if false?

## Actors And Incentives

- Who has power to change this?
- Who benefits from the current state?
- Who bears the cost?
- What does each person or group seem to be optimizing for?
- What is unsaid because saying it directly would be costly?

## Technical Context

- What is the system boundary?
- Where does the failing request, data, or state flow?
- What changed in code, config, dependency, traffic, or environment?
- Is the failure deterministic or intermittent?
- What logs, tests, traces, or artifacts are available?

## Product Or Business Context

- Who is the customer?
- What job are they hiring this product or service to do?
- Is the bottleneck acquisition, activation, retention, monetization, trust, or differentiation?
- What substitute are users choosing today?
- What metric would prove the strategy is working?

## Career Or Learning Context

- What are you optimizing for: income, learning, autonomy, prestige, stability, location, mission, or optionality?
- What skill or asset would compound if you invested in it for a year?
- Where can you get fast feedback?
- What proof of ability can you create?
- What path closes doors you care about?

## Conflict Or Organization Context

- Is this disagreement about facts, priorities, authority, trust, incentives, or personality?
- Who owns the decision?
- What would make the other side's behavior rational?
- What conversation has not happened yet?
- What agreement needs to be made explicit?

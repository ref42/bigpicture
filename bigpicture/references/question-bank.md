# Question Bank

Ask fewer questions than seem tempting. Prefer one high-value question over a checklist.

## Problem Shape

- What outcome are you actually trying to get?
- What changed recently that made this a problem now?
- Is this a one-time event or a repeating pattern?
- What have you already tried?
- What would count as a good enough resolution?

## Constraints

- What cannot change?
- What resources are limited: time, money, attention, authority, trust, data, skill, or distribution?
- What deadline matters?
- What happens if you do nothing?
- Which option is reversible?

## Stakes And Risk

- What is the downside if this goes wrong?
- Who else is affected?
- Is the cost mostly financial, reputational, emotional, technical, legal, or operational?
- What would make you regret the decision later?
- What assumption would be most damaging if false?

## Actors And Incentives

- Who has power to change this?
- Who benefits from the current state?
- Who bears the cost?
- What does each person or group seem to be optimizing for?
- What is unsaid because saying it directly would be costly?

## Technical Context

- What is the system boundary?
- Where does the failing request, data, or state flow?
- What changed in code, config, dependency, traffic, or environment?
- Is the failure deterministic or intermittent?
- What logs, tests, traces, or artifacts are available?

## Product Or Business Context

- Who is the customer?
- What job are they hiring this product or service to do?
- Is the bottleneck acquisition, activation, retention, monetization, trust, or differentiation?
- What substitute are users choosing today?
- What metric would prove the strategy is working?

## Career Or Learning Context

- What are you optimizing for: income, learning, autonomy, prestige, stability, location, mission, or optionality?
- What skill or asset would compound if you invested in it for a year?
- Where can you get fast feedback?
- What proof of ability can you create?
- What path closes doors you care about?

## Conflict Or Organization Context

- Is this disagreement about facts, priorities, authority, trust, incentives, or personality?
- Who owns the decision?
- What would make the other side's behavior rational?
- What conversation has not happened yet?
- What agreement needs to be made explicit?

## Systems And Feedback Context

- What is accumulating or being depleted in this situation — trust, technical debt, morale, cash, capability?
- Is the problem getting worse on its own, or is it stable without intervention?
- What happens after the obvious first result of an action — who adapts, what expectation is set, what dependency is created?
- Is there a delay between cause and visible effect that might be causing overreaction or oscillation?
- Is the current fix addressing the symptom, the immediate cause, or the structure that keeps producing both?
- Does the fix depend on someone always doing the right thing, or does the structure make the right thing automatic?
- Are you adjusting a number, or changing a rule, a goal, or a shared assumption?

## Engineering Scale And Evolution

- How many people will maintain this, and for how long?
- What behaviors of this system will users depend on that are not in the documented contract?
- Is this decision reversible at low cost, or hard to undo once made?
- What is the cost of this choice per year, multiplied by the number of people affected?
- Is there a plan for how to retire or replace this, or only a plan for how to build it?
- Does the current tooling make the correct path the path of least resistance, or does correctness require ongoing discipline?
- What would a change to this look like in two years, when the original author is no longer available?

## Team Culture And Environment

- Is this problem technical, or is it actually social — about communication, incentives, environment, or trust?
- Are people being asked to do more with less time, or is the bottleneck in how work is structured?
- Is the team stable, or has it been recently reorganized or split?
- Are errors being surfaced and examined openly, or are they being hidden?
- Does the environment protect uninterrupted concentration, or is focus constantly interrupted?
- Are the people doing the work the same people making the decisions about how it gets done?

## Design And Usability Context

- Does the user know what actions are available to them at this point?
- After taking an action, does the user know what happened and whether it worked?
- Is the difficulty coming from not knowing how to do something, or from misunderstanding what the system is doing?
- When something goes wrong, is the cause clear and the recovery path obvious?
- Does the design communicate the correct mental model, or does it invite a wrong one?
- Is the error more likely a slip — correct intent, wrong execution — or a mistake — wrong understanding of the situation?
